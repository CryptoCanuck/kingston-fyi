## Decisions (this brief)

- **2026-05-30 — Working mode:** Coaching path (walk section by section, push on thin spots).
- **2026-05-30 — OQ1a Launch content:** Curated + aggregated (seed directory/events from
  public sources + light original editorial; curate everything). Aligns with avoiding the
  unsustainable daily-news treadmill that sinks solo hyperlocal founders.
- **2026-05-30 — OQ1b Monetization (shapes data model now):** Featured/promoted listings +
  Sponsored content & ads + Business claim subscriptions. (NOT "none at launch.")
  → Open tension to coach: sponsored/display ads are sales-labour-heavy and slow for a
    small team (Patch lesson); directory claim/featured + newsletter sponsorship scale better.
- **2026-05-30 — OQ2 Geo scope:** Multi-city ready — Kingston launches first, but city/
  tenancy baked into the model + taxonomies from the start (no rewrite to expand).
- **2026-05-31 — Capacity & runway:** Solo founder for now (the only operator), self-funded
  posture implied; intends to **expand the team down the road, tied to launching additional
  city domains** (the multi-domain `.fyi` family — Kingston first). Expansion vector = more
  cities on shared platform infra, team grows with revenue.
  → **Design constraint (runs through the whole brief): solo-operable / automation-first.**
    The MVP must be runnable by one person — heavy reliance on scrape-seeding the directory,
    self-serve claim/submission + a low-overhead moderation queue, minimal manual content load.
- **2026-05-31 — Monetization phasing (reframe, pending user confirm):** Lead with **self-serve,
  recurring** revenue that scales without a salesperson — featured/promoted listings + business
  claim subscriptions + newsletter sponsorship. **Sponsored content & display ads → Phase 2**,
  unlocked when traffic + a dedicated sales hand exist (per Village Media / Patch evidence).
  Data model still accommodates all three from the start.
- **2026-05-31 — Monetization refinement (AdSense):** Add **Google AdSense (programmatic
  display)** to the **launch** mix — it's self-serve/passive (revenue ∝ traffic, no sales
  labour), so unlike bespoke ad sales it fits solo ops. Launch passive layer now =
  featured/promoted listings + claim subscriptions + newsletter sponsorship + AdSense.
  Direct/sponsored local ad *sales* stays Phase 2. Caveats baked into brief: (a) hyperlocal
  AdSense CPMs are low → baseline/gravy, NOT primary revenue (directory still leads); (b)
  must be CWV- & brand-disciplined (few units, lazy-load, reserved slots, no interstitials)
  to honor the locked CWV + civic-trust guardrails; (c) AdSense's no-ads-on-thin-content
  policy reinforces the "broad but genuinely useful" quality guardrail.
- **2026-05-31 — Success metrics:** Pageviews = vanity. Two tiers — **Leading/habit:** organic
  sessions, indexed pages (SEO surface area), newsletter subs + open rate, returning-visitor
  share, events engagement. **Flywheel/revenue:** directory density, **claimed listings (the
  week-to-week steering metric — agreed)**, first paying businesses, **AdSense RPM/revenue**.
  Specific 6/12-month numeric targets = TBD (honest unknown; offer directional placeholders).

- **2026-05-31 — News sourcing (resolved a flagged risk):** Do NOT scrape/rewrite Kingstonist
  or any competitor's (paywalled) articles — copyright/fair-dealing exposure, brand-trust
  suicide in a small market, and Google "scaled content abuse" demotion. Competitors are a
  **signal/research bucket only** (what to cover, what the community cares about). Published
  content comes from an **automated primary-source + aggregation engine**: institutional press
  releases (City, Queen's, SLC, KFL&A, Police, Frontenacs), public data/feeds, legit link-out
  aggregation ("Kingston in 5"), and AI assisting the founder's *own* drafts from primary sources.
- **2026-05-31 — Positioning wedge (news):** **Free / no paywall.** Community frustration with
  Kingstonist's paywall (per social sentiment) makes open access a deliberate differentiator —
  reinforces civic-trust brand, fits the free + ad/sponsor model, and is SEO-friendlier.

## Cold-start theory (2026-05-31, confirmed)

Flywheel: **scrape-seed directory to city-wide density** (useful from minute one, nobody pays
yet) → **events + "near me" + free news as the resident front door** (cleanest SEO + immediate
utility + daily habit) → **social distributes** → audience makes listings valuable → **convert
businesses to paid claims** once there's traffic to point at. Automation farms content so one
person can sustain it.

## MVP cut (2026-05-31, confirmed)

**Option A — all three pillars, cross-linked from day one.** The cross-link is the entire
differentiator, so all three must be present at launch. Framing: **light on manual effort,
broad on automated coverage** — SEO surface area is the traffic strategy, and automation is
what lets one person be broad. Directory scrape-seeded to real density + claim flow; events
aggregated + submission; news free/no-paywall from primary sources (manual original volume
starts low, automated/aggregated coverage as broad as quality allows) + the "Kingston in 5"
newsletter; cross-linking + moderation queue + SEO/JSON-LD from the start. Multi-city-ready
model, Kingston live first.
**Quality guardrail:** broad coverage must be *genuinely useful* pages (real listing/event
data, real primary-source reporting) — never thin auto-filler (Google scaled-content-abuse).

## Landscape research (2026-05-30) — key findings

Full digest: `research-landscape.md`. Headlines:
- **Open lane confirmed:** No Kingston incumbent offers a city-wide, structured, *claimable*
  news+events+directory hub. Kingstonist = editorial cross-reference only; Downtown Kingston
  BIA does the integrated model well but **downtown-members only**; **KingstonToday does not
  exist** (Village Media has no Kingston site — verify domain before finalizing).
- **Primary competitor:** Kingstonist (trusted, QCJO, already news+events+lifestyle). Don't
  win by out-reporting; win on the **structured/claimable directory + events utility + cross-link**.
- **Monetization reality:** Village Media model = direct local display ads (labour-heavy) +
  newsletter sponsorship + small directory; profitable but **2–3 yrs to profitability**.
  Patch lesson: hand-selling small ads doesn't scale → favor self-serve directory/claim
  tiers + newsletter sponsorship + event-promo fees.
- **Directory moat = accuracy + curation + local context** (can't beat Google head-on;
  win by being what Google isn't). Seed via scrape → convert owners via claim flow (gates premium).
- **SEO leverage:** Event JSON-LD ("near me" carousel above Map Pack) + LocalBusiness NAP +
  news SEO; cross-linking itself builds topical authority.
- **Top risk:** solo/small-team runway + content treadmill + ad-sales labour.
